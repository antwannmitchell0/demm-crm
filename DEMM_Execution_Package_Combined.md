# DEMM Ecosystem Constitution
## Version 3.0 — Green-Lit Operating Constitution

**Owner:** Antwann Mitchell, Chairman, DEMM LLC  
**Status:** Approved for execution  
**Effective date:** July 21, 2026  
**Applies to:** DEMM Marketing, DEMM Photo Booths, WTAE, GREATER, SOFTER, Be Great, and the DEMM intelligence stack

---

## 1. Authority and Purpose

This Constitution is the highest-level operating document for DEMM LLC.

It defines:

- why the ecosystem exists;
- which businesses are canonical;
- how they reinforce one another;
- how money, customers, attention, and intelligence move through the ecosystem;
- which decisions are locked;
- how future decisions are approved.

Technical plans, product requirements, agent instructions, workflows, and releases must conform to this Constitution.

When a lower-level document conflicts with this Constitution, this Constitution controls unless Antwann approves a formal amendment.

---

## 2. What DEMM Is

DEMM LLC is one ecosystem with five operating pillars and one unifying movement.

DEMM helps people grow, helps businesses grow, captures community, and turns attention into opportunity.

The ecosystem is designed to combine:

- business growth;
- immediate and recurring revenue;
- live experiences;
- community attention;
- personal transformation;
- artificial intelligence;
- ethical, consent-based intelligence.

DEMM is not five unrelated startups. Each business has its own customers, economics, data boundaries, and operating model, but every business must strengthen at least one other part of the ecosystem.

---

## 3. Mission

Use technology, artificial intelligence, marketing, media, live experiences, and human connection to create stronger businesses, stronger people, stronger families, and more opportunity.

---

## 4. Vision

Build the most trusted growth and community ecosystem serving Black entrepreneurs, professionals, families, creators, and communities—beginning in Atlanta and expanding nationally.

A person should be able to enter through inspiration, business ownership, an event, a photograph, a personal challenge, or a relationship and discover additional value without being manipulated, over-targeted, or having private information misused.

---

## 5. Financial Trajectory

The active trajectory is:

1. **$45,000 in revenue within 90 days**
2. **$1,000,000 in annual recurring revenue**
3. **$10,000,000+ in annual recurring revenue**
4. **National ecosystem expansion**

The 90-day target is an execution target, not a promise. Every major product or engineering initiative must identify:

- the revenue milestone it supports;
- the customer outcome it improves;
- the metric that will prove value;
- the cost of delay.

No feature is automatically justified because it is innovative.

## 7. The Five Pillars

### 7.1 DEMM Marketing — Growth and Systems Engine

**Purpose:** Build businesses.

DEMM Marketing helps service businesses and organizations capture leads, follow up, close sales, onboard customers, operate consistently, and measure results.

It provides:

- CRM and pipeline systems;
- websites and funnels;
- AI agents and automations;
- lead capture and follow-up;
- marketing strategy and execution;
- customer onboarding;
- reporting and optimization.

**Current reality**

- Founder-led.
- Delivery scope is not yet standardized.
- Founder pricing exists at **$99, $299, and $999 per month**.
- Current reference industries include lawn care and salon suites.
- Antwann is currently the primary operator.

**Future state**

- Productized offers.
- Clear service-level agreements.
- Repeatable onboarding.
- Agent-supported fulfillment.
- White-label and licensing opportunities.
- Antwann operates as Chairman and rainmaker rather than sole technician.

**Revenue role:** Immediate and recurring revenue.

---

### 7.2 DEMM Photo Booths — Atlanta Entry Vehicle Becoming a Cash Engine

**Purpose now:** Get DEMM into rooms, generate immediate revenue, build relationships, create content, and open doors in Atlanta.

**Purpose later:** Become an operator-managed, systemized event business that produces recurring cash without requiring Antwann to personally operate every event.

**Current reality**

- Active and accepting bookings.
- Two owned units.
- The 360 booth is being removed from active service.
- Antwann currently operates events.
- Typical booking value is approximately **$500**, subject to package and market validation.
- Photo Booths is not yet passive or operator-managed.

**Future state**

- Trained operators.
- Documented setup and breakdown procedures.
- Equipment maintenance and backup procedures.
- Automated lead response, quoting, contracting, payment reminders, and review collection.
- Deep integration with WTAE galleries and event follow-up.

**Revenue role:** Fast cash and customer acquisition.

The Constitution must never describe the future operator model as if it already exists.

---

### 7.3 WTAE — Attention, Experience, and Event Revenue Platform

**Purpose:** Turn live moments into ongoing attention, connection, community, and measurable value for organizers, attendees, photographers, sponsors, and businesses.

WTAE is not merely a photography company. Photography is an acquisition mechanism.

The WTAE experience is:

**Event → Capture → Discover → Claim → Share → Recognize → Return**

**Current reality**

- WTAE is a product under DEMM LLC.
- Antwann is preparing and operating early pilots.
- Initial organizer pricing is being tested.
- Antwann currently supplies photography and will build the photographer team.
- The product requires operational systems designed for rapid growth.

**Revenue model**

- event organizer fees;
- premium activation packages;
- sponsor integrations;
- recurring organizer plans;
- photographer and creator services;
- branded experiences;
- future analytics and memberships.

**North-star metric:** Qualified Moments Activated per Event.

**Revenue role:** Event revenue now; recurring platform and sponsor revenue later.

**Attention role:** Offline-to-online acquisition for the broader ecosystem.

Any internally reported event fee or provisioned event must be verified in the canonical system before being used as official pricing evidence.

---

### 7.4 GREATER — Transformation Platform for Men

**Purpose:** Help men become greater through accountability, reflection, brotherhood, memory, and consistent support.

MARCUS is the primary AI companion and must remain culturally grounded, trustworthy, and disciplined.

**Current reality**

- No confirmed paid subscribers.
- Monetization and acquisition are not yet proven.
- Marketing investment depends on revenue from nearer-term businesses.
- A formal crisis-response standard operating procedure is required.

**Revenue options**

- consumer subscriptions;
- annual memberships;
- corporate wellness;
- speaking and community partnerships;
- grants or institutional funding where appropriate;

**Operational relationship:** GREATER is both a DEMM pillar and an internal client of DEMM Marketing.

### 7.5 SOFTER — Transformation Platform for Women

**Purpose:** Help Black women choose themselves through reflection, wellness practices, community, and an AI companion designed to hold space without pretending to replace licensed care.

ARIA is the primary AI companion.

**Current reality**

**Revenue options**

**Operational relationship:** SOFTER is both a DEMM pillar and an internal client of DEMM Marketing.

## 11. Internal-Client Model

GREATER and SOFTER are clients of DEMM Marketing.

WTAE and DEMM Photo Booths may also consume DEMM Marketing services.

This creates a proof loop:

1. DEMM Marketing builds systems internally.
2. Internal businesses use those systems.
3. Results and failures are measured.
4. The systems are improved.
5. Proven capabilities are sold externally.

Internal businesses must not receive fake reports or vanity metrics. They are test environments, but they must still operate with real service expectations.

## 12. Cross-Business Handoffs

### Photo Booths → WTAE

- Photo Booth bookings receive WTAE gallery and engagement capabilities where appropriate.

- WTAE becomes a differentiator for Photo Booth packages.

### WTAE → DEMM Marketing

- Organizers needing registration, follow-up, sponsor activation, websites, CRM, or post-event marketing become qualified DEMM Marketing opportunities.

### DEMM Marketing → WTAE

- DEMM Marketing builds organizer funnels, event communications, sponsor follow-up, and reporting systems.
- DEMM Marketing treats WTAE as an internal client.

### WTAE → GREATER and SOFTER

- WTAE may present optional, relevant introductions to GREATER or SOFTER.
- No sensitive inference or automatic enrollment is permitted.
- The attendee chooses whether to continue.

### GREATER and SOFTER → WTAE

- Members may learn about relevant community events.
- Participation is optional.
- Private conversations and wellness data never become event targeting data.

## 14. Community Intelligence and Consent

The Community Intelligence Network is a strategic capability, not an excuse to combine all data.

Rules:

1. Each business maintains a clear purpose for collecting data.
2. Sensitive wellness conversations, journals, cycle data, crisis information, and private reflections remain isolated.
3. Cross-business transfer is denied by default.
4. A user must receive clear notice and make a meaningful choice before data is used across businesses.
5. Aggregated insights must be de-identified and reviewed for re-identification risk.
6. No attractiveness ratings, exploitative scoring, hidden targeting, or pay-to-win recognition.
7. WTAE recognition must remain aligned with the Give Flowers framework.
8. Biometric or face-matching features require explicit opt-in, retention limits, deletion controls, legal review, and a non-biometric alternative.
9. Until consent architecture is implemented, the Community Intelligence Network remains a future-state capability.

Trust is a permanent asset. Revenue never overrides consent.

---

## 15. Current State, Near-Term State, and Future State

Every roadmap must classify work as:

- **Current truth:** operating and verified today.
- **Near-term commitment:** approved and scheduled.
- **Future state:** desired but not yet funded, staffed, or proven.
- **Exploration:** an option requiring validation.

No system, report, or agent may describe a future state as completed.

---

## 16. Product and Engineering Rules

Every build must answer:

1. Which customer problem does this solve?
2. Which revenue or mission metric does it improve?
3. Which pillar owns it?
4. Which other pillar benefits?
5. What is the smallest usable release?
6. What evidence will prove it works?
7. What sensitive data is involved?
8. Can Antwann eventually step away from daily operation?
9. Are we building a real capability or a demonstration?
10. Does it conform to this Constitution?

No hardcoded production URLs.  
No fake AI labeled as real AI.  
No simulated healing, reporting, or intelligence presented as operational.  
No “complete” status without deployment and acceptance evidence.

---

## 17. Amendment Process

A constitutional amendment requires:

1. a written proposal;
2. the reason for the change;
3. businesses and systems affected;
4. risks and migration impact;
5. review by COUNCIL;
6. contradiction review by DOM26 and gbrain where available;
7. final approval from Antwann;
8. a new version number and dated ADR.

# DEMM Platform Blueprint
## Version 1.0 — Product and Operating Architecture

**Owner:** DEMM LLC  
**Authority:** DEMM Ecosystem Constitution v3.0  
**Status:** Approved design baseline  
**Primary implementation target:** Antigravity and the DEMM engineering workflow

---

## 1. Blueprint Objective

The DEMM Platform is a shared operating system for five businesses.

It is not five separate CRMs and not one undifferentiated database.

The platform must provide:

- a shared security and identity foundation;
- business-specific workspaces;
- reusable modules;
- explicit data boundaries;
- shared reporting;
- agent execution with approvals and auditability;
- a controlled executive intelligence layer.

---

## 2. Canonical Hierarchy

```text
DEMM LLC
└── Organization
    ├── Business Units
    │   ├── DEMM Marketing
    │   ├── DEMM Photo Booths
    │   ├── WTAE
    │   ├── GREATER
    │   └── SOFTER
    ├── Workspaces
    ├── Shared Modules
    ├── Business-Specific Modules
    ├── Agents
    └── Intelligence Layer
```

### Organization

The legal and executive boundary: DEMM LLC.

### Business Unit

A canonical business with its own:

- customers;
- offers;
- workflows;
- revenue;
- dashboards;
- permissions;
- data policy.

### Workspace

An operating subdivision inside a business unit.

Examples:

- DEMM Marketing / Internal Operations
- DEMM Marketing / Client: Lawn Care
- Photo Booths / Atlanta Operations
- WTAE / Event: Atlanta Pilot
- GREATER / Product Operations
- SOFTER / Product Operations

### Module

A reusable capability such as CRM, bookings, events, contracts, communications, payments, gallery management, subscriptions, analytics, or agents.

---

## 3. Shared Foundation

The shared foundation must support every business.

### Required foundation capabilities

- authentication;
- refresh-token rotation;
- role-based access;
- organization, business-unit, and workspace scoping;
- audit logs;
- approvals;
- tasks;
- contacts and companies;
- opportunities and pipelines;
- configuration validation;
- secret management;
- version and release identity;
- observability;
- backups;
- integration registry;
- notification service.

### Non-negotiable foundation rules

- Every record has an owning business unit.
- Workspace access does not imply access to all business units.
- Sensitive modules may apply stronger access rules.
- Cross-business visibility is summary-first and permission-controlled.
- Production configuration is environment-based.
- Every deployed build exposes version, commit, environment, and build time.
- Every write action by an agent is auditable.
- High-risk actions require approval.

## 5. Cross-Business Person Model

A Person may interact with multiple businesses, but the system must not assume that one relationship authorizes another.

Example:

- The same person may be a WTAE attendee.
- They may later become a DEMM Marketing lead.
- They may separately join GREATER or SOFTER.

The platform may maintain a shared identity key, but each relationship requires:

- a business-specific profile;
- purpose limitation;
- consent status;
- communication preferences;
- deletion and retention rules.

Default: no cross-business marketing enrollment.

Release 1.0 must turn the existing CRM foundation into a verifiable operating platform for the businesses closest to revenue and Atlanta entry.

It must deliver a usable vertical slice for:

1. DEMM Marketing
2. DEMM Photo Booths

GREATER and SOFTER must exist as canonical business units and internal client profiles, but their full consumer-product features are outside this release.

- capture and manage DEMM Marketing leads;
- sell and onboard founder-tier clients;

- connect Photo Booth gallery delivery to WTAE;

## 5. Phase 0 — Truth and Recovery

### Objective

Establish one canonical repository, one verifiable deployment path, and a working frontend-to-backend connection.

### Required tasks

1. Identify the canonical repository and production branch.
2. Record current HEAD commit.
3. Inventory duplicate DEMM CRM repositories and classify them.
4. Determine the actual Cloud Run project and required access.
5. Replace hardcoded `http://localhost:3001/api` in the production frontend path.
6. Use environment-based API configuration.
7. Add a safe production guard that fails the build if localhost is embedded.
8. Add version metadata:
   - app version;
   - Git commit;
   - build timestamp;
   - environment.
9. Confirm backend `/health`.
10. Confirm backend `/version`.
11. Confirm frontend login loads.
12. Confirm frontend can call the deployed backend.
13. Preserve screenshots, logs, URLs, commit, and build evidence.

### Acceptance criteria

- Production bundle contains no localhost API reference.
- Frontend successfully reaches backend.
- `/version` returns a real commit rather than `UNKNOWN_COMMIT`.
- A documented command or pipeline reproduces the deployment.
- The release evidence package identifies the exact deployed commit.

No business feature work begins until the frontend-backend path is verified.

## 6. Phase 1 — Business Unit Foundation

### Objective

Add the five-pillar operating model without breaking existing tenant, workspace, security, or audit behavior.

### Required capabilities

- BusinessUnit entity.
- Workspace belongs to BusinessUnit.
- Existing organization remains DEMM LLC.
- Migration assigns existing records to a default business unit.
- Business-unit selector in the authenticated UI.
- Business-unit scoping in backend services.
- Permission checks.
- Audit events include business unit and workspace.
- Seed the five canonical business units.
- Create internal client profiles for GREATER and SOFTER.

### Migration principles

- Additive first.
- Backfill before enforcing non-null constraints.
- Preserve existing IDs.
- No destructive rename without compatibility layer.
- Provide rollback steps.
- Verify record counts before and after migration.

### Acceptance criteria

- A user with Marketing-only access cannot read Photo Booth or WTAE records.
- Executive roles can view approved summaries across business units.
- Existing CRM data remains accessible.
- Audit logs show business-unit scope.
